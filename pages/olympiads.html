Olympiad preparation

Train like an economist, write like a finalist.

Olympiad success comes from model fluency, careful diagrams, clean assumptions, and fast reflection after mistakes.

12-week roadmap

A realistic plan for students balancing school, contests, and deep study.

Weeks 1-2: Foundations

Scarcity, opportunity cost, demand, supply, GDP, inflation, and graph hygiene. Solve short drills daily.

Weeks 3-5: Core models

Elasticity, market structures, fiscal policy, monetary policy, exchange rates, and AD-AS. Write one paragraph explanation after every graph.

Weeks 6-8: Advanced reasoning

Game theory, externalities, IS-LM, growth, inequality, and policy evaluation. Practice mixed sets under time pressure.

Weeks 9-12: Contest simulation

Full mock papers, post-mortems, formula recall, and essay outlines. Track recurring errors and rebuild weak models.

Problem-solving technique

Read the final question first, define variables, draw the model, mark the shock, then calculate. Never let arithmetic lead the economics.

Common mistakes

Confusing movement along a curve with curve shifts, mixing nominal and real values, ignoring ceteris paribus, and using memorized policy without diagnosing the gap.

Recommended books

Use one clean introductory economics text, one problem book, and one policy-oriented news source. Depth beats a long unread list.

Weekly rhythm

Three concept sessions, two timed problem sessions, one essay session, and one review block. Keep a mistake journal.

Essay scoring

Strong essays define the model, explain causality, evaluate limitations, and end with a precise trade-off.

Winners culture

Finalists are not people who never miss questions. They are people who convert misses into reusable rules.
